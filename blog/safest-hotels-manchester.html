Is Manchester Safe for Tourists?

Manchester's city centre is vibrant and largely safe ‚Äî but postcode matters when choosing your hotel

Manchester is safe for tourists who choose their accommodation wisely. The city centre, Spinningfields, Deansgate, and the First Street area all have strong safety records and are where the vast majority of visitors stay. Problems tend to occur in outer residential areas ‚Äî Moss Side, Hulme, and parts of Salford ‚Äî that tourists have no reason to visit.

Petty crime, particularly phone snatching and pickpocketing around Piccadilly Gardens, is the most frequent complaint from visitors. The city's nightlife areas around Deansgate Locks and the Northern Quarter are lively but generally well-managed, with visible door staff and good lighting on main routes.

üí° Key fact: Piccadilly Gardens has seen investment and improved policing in recent years, but remains an area where you should keep valuables secure and stay aware of your surroundings, particularly in the evenings.

Spinningfields is consistently our top-rated Manchester area. This modern financial district has exceptionally low crime rates, immaculate streets, and some of Manchester's finest hotels and restaurants. It's our top recommendation for anyone prioritising safety.

Castlefield is a beautiful area built around Manchester's Victorian canal network. It feels like a village within the city ‚Äî low crime, great restaurants, and a genuinely relaxed atmosphere. Hotels here are excellent value compared to Spinningfields for a similar safety profile.

Salford Quays and MediaCityUK are worth considering if you don't mind being slightly out of the centre (a short tram ride). The area has seen massive regeneration and has very good crime statistics.

Safety Tips for Visiting Manchester

  • Use the Metrolink tram ‚Äî it's safe, cheap, and connects all the main areas including the airport and Salford Quays
  • Piccadilly Gardens in the evening ‚Äî keep moving through here rather than lingering; it's a well-known spot for street drinkers
  • The Northern Quarter is brilliant but stay aware late at night ‚Äî it's generally safe but is a busy nightlife area
  • Always pre-book taxis or use Uber ‚Äî licensed black cabs and the app are reliable; avoid unlicensed taxis especially after nights out
  • Keep your phone in your pocket on Deansgate and Market Street ‚Äî phone theft is the most common crime affecting tourists
  • Book hotels with 24hr reception ‚Äî essential for late check-ins off the trains from London Euston
