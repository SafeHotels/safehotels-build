Birmingham Safety: The Big Picture

Birmingham's canal network and revitalised city centre are popular with tourists and generally very safe

Birmingham is a city of contrasts. The West Midlands as a whole has higher crime rates than the national average, but this is heavily concentrated in specific residential areas that tourists rarely visit. The city centre and popular tourist districts tell a very different story.

The city hosted the 2022 Commonwealth Games without significant safety incidents, welcoming millions of visitors from around the world. This was a significant moment that demonstrated Birmingham's capacity to be a safe, well-managed destination at scale.

Areas to Be More Cautious In

Every major city has areas that are less suitable for tourists, and Birmingham is no different. The following areas have higher recorded crime rates and few tourist attractions ‚Äî there's simply no need to stay in these parts of the city when safer central options are available at similar or lower prices.

Handsworth, Lozells, and Newtown are residential areas with significantly higher crime rates than the city centre. They're not destinations any tourist guide would send you to, and our data confirms they have the lowest safety scores in our Birmingham database.

Small Heath and Bordesley Green are similarly residential areas that score lower on our safety index. Again, no tourist would have a reason to base themselves here.
