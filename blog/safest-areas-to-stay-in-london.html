The 5 Safest Areas to Stay in London

98/100

1. Richmond upon Thames

Consistently the lowest-crime London borough in official statistics. Leafy, affluent, and packed with green space along the Thames. Ideal for families and those who want suburban calm with good transport into central London. The crime rate here is roughly one-third of the London average.

96/100

2. Kensington & Chelsea

Home to some of London's most prestigious addresses, world-class museums (the V&A, Natural History Museum), and Harrods. Well-lit streets, high visible police presence, and a predominantly affluent residential population keep crime extremely low. An excellent base for first-time visitors.

95/100

3. Hampstead & Camden (North)

Hampstead itself is a charming, village-like area with one of London's largest parks. While Camden Town is livelier and worth some caution at night, Hampstead and surrounding NW3 postcodes are exceptionally safe and pleasant for solo travellers and families alike.

94/100

4. Greenwich

Southeast London's heritage gem, home to the Cutty Sark, the Royal Observatory, and the beautiful Greenwich Park. The tourist areas around the market and park are very safe. A great option if you're visiting the O2 Arena or travelling via the Elizabeth line.

93/100

5. Marylebone & Fitzrovia (W1/W1U)

Central London doesn't have to mean high crime. Marylebone ‚Äî home to the famous Baker Street ‚Äî is one of the safest central postcodes with excellent restaurants, boutique shopping, and convenient access to the West End. Fitzrovia, just east, offers similar safety with a creative, neighbourhood feel.

Areas to Be More Cautious In

London's reputation for danger is frequently overstated in foreign media. Even its highest-crime areas are safe by global standards. That said, some postcodes warrant a little more awareness, especially at night.

Peckham and parts of South East London have seen elevated knife crime in recent years. As a tourist, you're unlikely to be a target, but staying alert after dark is sensible. The excellent transport links mean you rarely need to be anywhere deserted.

Paddington Station's immediate surroundings have higher petty theft rates ‚Äî predominantly pickpocketing. Hotels right on the station are convenient but keep a close eye on belongings in crowds.

Oxford Street is one of London's highest-crime areas for pickpocketing due to the sheer volume of tourists. It's perfectly safe to visit; just use a secure bag and don't keep a wallet in your back pocket.

Practical Safety Tips for London

  • Use the Tube after midnight: The Night Tube runs on Fridays and Saturdays on key lines. It's a safe, well-policed alternative to night buses or walking.
  • Book licensed taxis or Uber: Don't accept rides from unlicensed drivers, especially near nightlife areas.
  • Choose hotels with 24-hour reception: Particularly important for solo female travellers. Our database filters by this.
  • Check the postcode, not just the hotel name: A "central London" hotel could be in SW1 (excellent) or E1 (mixed). Always check the postcode on our safety map before booking.
  • Keep emergency numbers handy: UK police non-emergency: 101. Emergency: 999. The Met Police also have a text service for those with hearing difficulties.
